Boracay

Malay, Aklan, Western Visayas, Philippines.

Known as one of the world's top island destinations, Boracay is famous for its powdery white sand beaches, crystal-clear turquoise waters, and vibrant nightlife. It offers a wide range of water sports activities, from snorkeling and diving to parasailing and kiteboarding. Visitors can also indulge in delicious seafood cuisine and explore the island's bustling markets and lively beach clubs.

Puerto
Princesa

Palawan, MIMAROPA, Philippines.

Puerto Princesa, the capital city of Palawan, is home to the stunning Puerto Princesa Underground River, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the New7Wonders of Nature. Siargao

Surigao del Norte, Caraga, Philippines.

Siargao Island is renowned for its world-class surfing waves, attracting surfers from around the globe to spots like Cloud 9. Beyond surfing, Siargao boasts pristine beaches, turquoise lagoons, and mangrove forests waiting to be explored. The island's laid-back vibe, friendly locals, and delicious seafood cuisine add to its charm, making it a must-visit destination in the Philippines.

Bohol

Central Visayas, Philippines.

Bohol is famous for its unique geological formations, including the iconic Chocolate Hills, as well as its adorable tarsiers, one of the world's smallest primates. Visitors can also explore historic landmarks like the Blood Compact Shrine and Baclayon Church, relax on white sand beaches, and enjoy activities such as river cruising and diving in vibrant coral reefs.

Cebu

Cebu Province, Central Visayas, Philippines.

Cebu is a bustling province rich in history, culture, and natural beauty. Its capital, Cebu City, is known for landmarks like Magellan's Cross and Fort San Pedro, while the province's coastline offers picturesque beaches and world-class diving spots. Cebu is also famous for its mouthwatering lechon (roast pig) and vibrant festivals, such as the Sinulog Festival.

Baguio

Tagaytay

Tagaytay City offers stunning views of Taal Volcano, one of the Philippines' most iconic natural landmarks. Bicol

Region V, Southeastern Luzon, Philippines.

The Bicol Region is known for its scenic landscapes, spicy cuisine, and natural attractions. It is home to the majestic Mayon Volcano, renowned for its perfect cone shape, as well as stunning beaches, hot springs, and whale shark watching in Donsol. Bicol's rich cultural heritage is celebrated through colorful festivals like the Penafrancia Festival in Naga City.

Vigan

Ilocos Sur, Ilocos Region, Luzon, Philippines.

Vigan City is renowned for its well-preserved Spanish colonial architecture, earning it a spot on the UNESCO World Heritage List. Visitors can stroll along cobblestone streets lined with ancestral houses, explore historic landmarks like the Vigan Cathedral and Plaza Salcedo, and shop for traditional crafts and souvenirs at the iconic Calle Crisologo. Vigan's cultural richness and charm make it a favorite destination for history enthusiasts and photographers.
